Elementary School Biology

What is an animal?

A living thing that eats, gives off wastes, reproduces, and moves around.

What is a plant?

Something that has life, can move, grow, and die.

Brain and Senses

Your brain is the most complex part of your body, it performs a large number of amazing calculations every second, taking in information from the outside world in the form of senses and allowing you to quickly respond to various situations.

We have 5 main senses

  • Smell

    • Your nose does a lot more than keep your glasses in place! It allows you to smell and to taste the food you eat. The nose is the beginning of your respiratory system – the system that allows you breathe. It also contains tiny hairs that help to keep you healthy by trapping dust and microbes. There are at least 10 million receptors in your nose!! Between them, these receptors can recognise about 10,000 different types of smells – some of them good and some of them very bad!

  • Hear

    • The sounds you hear every day are collected by your ears. Your ears transport these sounds to your brain so it can figure out what each sound is. Hearing helps you to learn and to communicate with everyone around you. Your ears also have another really important job – they stop you from falling over!

  • Taste

    • Your mouth is made up of the teeth and tongue and it does a lot more than just help you chew; it helps you to speak or sing, allows you to smile or frown and produces saliva that helps to prevent infection. The best part of your mouth though is your tongue because it allows you to taste the good things that you like to eat!

  • Touch

    • Your skin is an amazing organ and is very important in keeping you healthy. It is very tough and holds all your inside bits, inside! Your skin makes sweat that helps to protect you and oils to keep you healthy. Sweat also helps to regulate your body temperature. You touch lots and lots of different things every day and your skin can figure out what most of them are!

  • See

    • Your eyes are some of the hardest working parts of your body! They work from the very beginning of your day, right to the very end when you go to sleep. Your eyes are like cameras - they take images from the world around you and send the information to your brain. Your brain then figures out what you are looking at. There are lots of different parts to your eyes.

    Each one of them is really important in your everyday life. You use at least one of your five senses every moment of every day and they are on duty even when you are asleep! Your senses work together to let your brain know what is going on around you. They help to keep you safe by warning you of any danger.
