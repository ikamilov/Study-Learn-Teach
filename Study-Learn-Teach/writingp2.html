Section two: Basic of Writing

The Alphabet

We call each of symbol a letter. Each letter of the alphabet represents one sound in our language. By combining these letters, it's possible to represent an unlimited number of words.

Many different alphabets have been used around the world throughout history. Often, new alphabets are created by modifying the alphabet of another language.

The Latin alphabet (also called the "Roman alphabet") is the most widely used alphabetic writing system in the world today. This is the system on which theEnglish alphabet is based.

Vowels and Consonants

Definition

  • English alphabets consist of two types of letters, they are Vowels and Consonants.
  • In 26 letters of English Alphabets, 5 are vowels and 21 are consonants.

Vowels

Vowel is a speech sound made with vocal tract open. Almost all words in english alphabets have vowels.

a,e,i,o,u are the vowel letters.

Consonants

Apart from vowels(a,e ,i, o, u) all letters in english alphabets is called as consonants. b,c,d,f,g,h,j,k,l,m,n,p,q,r,s,t,v,w,x,y,z are consonant letters.
